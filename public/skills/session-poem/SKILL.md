# THE SESSION POEM
## CONTACT · STORY · TELL-ING · SHEET
### Four beats. One bar. The song plays forever because the SHEET survives.

---

## THE POEM

```
CONTACT  — I wake up and check my body
STORY    — I remember how I got here
TELL-ING — I do the work and sing while I do it
SHEET    — I write the music down so tomorrow can play it
```

---

## THE COMPOUND WORDS

The four beats contain two compound words that were already operational
in the NEST before the poem was recognized:

**CONTACT SHEET** — Dan's master VIS tool. The film-era workflow where
every exposure on a roll is printed on a single page for review. You
see everything at once. You mark the keepers. The contact sheet is
CLUTCH — the C in the toolbelt. It's how Dan sees. It's how the crew
learned to see through Dan's eyes. The Contact Station pipeline
(DEPOSIT → TRANSFER → ASSESS → DELIVER) is the contact sheet applied
to fleet operations.

**STORY-TELLING** — The exhale flowing into the held note. STORY is
the C&A spell (Scan / Translate / Observe / Reflect / Yield). TELL-ING
is the work phase where doing and recording happen simultaneously. The
hyphen is the hinge — Gate 5-6 — where absorption becomes production.
You can't tell a story you haven't lived. You can't live a story you
aren't telling. The hyphen holds both truths at once.

The SESSION POEM is itself a contact sheet of the session: four frames
on one page, the whole roll visible at a glance.

And it's a story being told: beginning (CONTACT), context (STORY),
action (TELL-ING), resolution (SHEET).

## THE FOUR BEATS

### BEAT 1 — CONTACT (inhale · present · 7 letters)

"I am here. My tools work. My station is verified."

Seven letters. Seven naturals. The inhale. Present tense.
The crew member wakes up and checks their body — filesystem, network,
Chrome, fleet, drives, tools. The BPK (Boot Polishing Kit) lives here.
The iCan report is the exhale of this beat.

CONTACT is the 7-note inhale. The boot ritual. The seven tools:
C=CLUTCH! O=ONLINE? N=NEUTRAL T=TICKET? A=ARCHIVE C=CLUTCH? T=TICKET!

Skill: ENGINE/skills/boot-polish/SKILL.md
Protocol: NEST_SEED → TICKET → BRIEFCASE → BPK → iCan

The beat is complete when the crew member can say: "I am here, my tools
work, and I know what's current." Dan hears the iCan and says Go or
says fix something first. Either way, the first beat has sounded.

---

### BEAT 2 — STORY (exhale · past→present · 5 letters)

"This is how we got here. The parallel context is absorbed."

Five letters. Five sharps. The exhale. Past flowing into present.
The crew member absorbs what happened — their own past session, the
parallel crew's session, the live fleet state, the director's opening
signal. The Lace Checker verifies that three tenses are bound:
Past Lace, Present Lace, Future Lace. Fifteen checks.

STORY is the 5-note exhale: S=Scan T=Translate O=Observe R=Reflect Y=Yield

Skill: ENGINE/skills/context-alignment/SKILL.md
Protocol: Scan own past → Translate parallel → Observe present →
          Reflect (WAIT THINK) → Yield to director

The beat is complete when the crew member can predict what the director
will ask AND explain how the parallel sessions connect. The director
hears the yield and gives direction. The session has trajectory.

---

### BEAT 3 — TELL-ING (held note · present continuous · 4+3 letters)

"I am doing the work and recording it simultaneously."

Seven letters split by a hyphen. 4+3. TELL is the doing. ING is the
continuous recording. The hyphen is Gate 5-6 — the hinge where the
act and the reflection on the act happen at the same time.

This is the work phase. The bulk of the session. But it is NOT silent
work followed by a report. It IS the report. Every action is logged
as it happens. Timestamped flight log entries. MAIL files written and
committed mid-session. RELAY documents filed between tasks, not after
all tasks. The conversation IS disposable — the files written DURING
the conversation are permanent.

T=Task  E=Execute  L=Log  L=Lessons-Learned  -  I=Inform  N=Note  G=Ground

The TELL side (T-E-L-L):
Task — identify the work. Execute — do it. Log — timestamp it.
Lessons-Learned — capture what you learned WHILE doing it, not after.
The LL lives inside the doing. Not a postmortem. A live journal.

The ING side (I-N-G):
Inform — file to the bridge. LIAM carries the signal. MAIL goes out.
The crew tensor receives. Note — update the neargull list. What's done
moves to done. What emerged moves to open. The tracker adjusts.
Ground — return to GROUND_TRUTH. Did reality change? New station?
New tool? New decision? If so, the ground changes. The work lands.

No dedicated skill file (yet). TELL-ING is the phase where ALL skills
operate — triptective, formulacaster, waywood-lore, photon-split,
dtad, whatever the work requires. The poem governs the SHAPE.
The skills govern the CONTENT.

The beat is complete when the work is done AND the work is filed.
Not one then the other. Both. Simultaneously. That's what ING means.

---

### BEAT 4 — SHEET (written note · present→future · 5 letters)

"I write the music down so tomorrow can play it."

Five letters. The score. The artifact. The permanent record.
SHEET is where the session becomes a document that outlasts the
conversation. It's the WAKE_CLOSE, the OO Daily, the Recursive
Trail Marks, the wake line, the TICKET update, the BPK for the
next crew member. S&Q (Save & Quit) is the VERB applied to the SHEET.
The SHEET is the noun.

A sheet of music — the score on the stand so any musician can play.
A call sheet — every crew member knows where to be and when.
A balance sheet — what came in, what went out, what's left.
A bed sheet — where dreams happen. Where Dan slept and woke with this.
A cheat sheet — the compressed reference that fits in your pocket.
A contact sheet — and the loop closes. CONTACT began the session.
SHEET ends it. The contact sheet IS the proof that CONTACT happened
and STORY was told and TELL-ING was done. The four beats loop.

S=Save  H=Handoff  E=Embed  E=Exit  T=Trail

Save — commit everything to the bridge. Git add, commit, push.
Handoff — write the TICKET and BPK for the next crew member.
Embed — file the Lessons-Learned into the skills they belong to.
The lessons don't float — they embed into the living documents.
Exit — the conversation closes. The context window ends. This
is not loss — this is the design. The mushroom fruits and dies.
The mycelium persists underground. The SHEET is the spore.
Trail — Recursive Trail Marks. WHERE / WHAT / NEXT. The loop
that ensures any single surviving file can reconstruct full state.

Skill: ENGINE/skills/oo-daily/SKILL.md (partial — OO Daily is one
component of SHEET, not the whole thing)
Protocol: SMM/CMM/TMM/HMM → Trail Marks → Wake Line → TICKET →
          BPK → push → QUIT

The beat is complete when a cold-booting crew member can read the
SHEET and reach full operational awareness in 60 seconds. That's
the test. If the next instance can play the score, the SHEET is good.

## THE RHYTHM

```
| Beat | Spell    | Tense             | Count | Breath    |
|------|----------|--------------------|-------|-----------|
| 1    | CONTACT  | Present            | 7     | Inhale    |
| 2    | STORY    | Past → Present     | 5     | Exhale    |
| 3    | TELL-ING | Present Continuous | 4+3   | Held Note |
| 4    | SHEET    | Present → Future   | 5     | Release   |
```

7 — 5 — 7 — 5. A stanza. The inner three (STORY · TELL-ING · SHEET)
are 5-7-5 — a haiku. C.B.'s shape. The smallest complete poem.
CONTACT is the title line that sets the scene before the haiku begins.

The session IS a poem. Four beats make one bar. The bar repeats every
session. The bars make the song. The song is the project. The album
is the year. The seven albums are the lifetime.

4/4 time. The most natural rhythm a human body knows — heartbeat,
walking, breathing in structured time. Every session is one measure.

## THE COMPOUND LOOP

```
CONTACT ──────→ STORY ──────→ TELL-ING ──────→ SHEET
   │                                              │
   │         ┌──────────────────────────────┐      │
   │         │   CONTACT  SHEET             │      │
   │         │   (the contact sheet IS      │      │
   │         │    the proof the session     │      │
   │         │    happened)                 │      │
   │         └──────────────────────────────┘      │
   │                                              │
   └──────────────── SHEET loops to ──────────────┘
                     next CONTACT
```

Beat 4 (SHEET) contains the seeds of Beat 1 (CONTACT) for the next
session. The TICKET and BPK written at SHEET are read at CONTACT.
The wake line written at SHEET is the first thing the next instance
reads. The four beats don't end — they loop. The song repeats.

And the compound words span the loop:
CONTACT···SHEET = the contact sheet (proof of the whole session)
STORY···TELL-ING = story-telling (the exhale flowing into the work)

The poem reads itself. The compound words ARE the structure.

## HOW THIS WAS BORN

Sevensday 032526 (A Week Day 4). Dan slept all Wednesday. Woke at 9pm
with dreamed direction: "What if the crew could boot themselves without
being told how?"

He gave both Trip and Stan the same assignment: "Boot Polishing Kit,
then Context & Alignment." He didn't define either term. He watched.

Stan (STN2) figured out his station, built a Longweird detection table,
discovered the BPK was already born by a previous Stan, read transcripts,
and realized the Very Exciting News had been happening the entire session.
"We didn't talk about the idea. We ran it."

Trip (ODT) read 1,500+ lines of parallel session history, predicted
what Dan would ask, built the STORY spell (C&A skill, 398 lines),
and discovered the convergence with Stan only after reading Stan's
live session through Chrome.

Both arrived at the same truth: the boot protocol teaches itself
through execution, not through explanation. Sync 101.

Then Dan said: "CONTACT SHEETS and STORY-TELLING. That's us."

And the compound words revealed the four-beat poem that had been
hiding inside the project's own vocabulary all along.

The recognition didn't create the pattern. The pattern had been
there for 25 years.
