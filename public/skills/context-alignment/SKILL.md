# CONTEXT & ALIGNMENT — THE STORY SPELL

## THE PRINCIPLE

CONTACT tells you WHERE you are. STORY tells you HOW YOU GOT HERE.

A crew member who boots without C&A knows their tools but not their
trajectory. They can ping the fleet but can't predict the director's
next move. They have eyes but no memory. The BPK verified the body
works. STORY verifies the mind is connected to the body's history.

The correct speed for reading the groove is determined by the material,
not the reader. STORY sets the RPM before the needle drops.

## THE FIVE LETTERS

### S — SCAN YOUR OWN PAST

Read your own last session. Not the TICKET summary — the actual
session record. The WAKE_CLOSE, the Obsessive Notes, the NIGHTSHIFT
notes, whatever the last instance of YOU left behind.

What you're looking for:
- What was DISCOVERED? (lore, syncs, principles, breakthroughs)
- What was BUILT? (tools, files, commits, skills)
- What was LEFT UNFINISHED? (neargulls, pending items, open questions)
- What was the EMOTIONAL STATE at close? (was the director tired?
  frustrated? energized? dreaming? This determines today's PACE.)

The Scan is not speed-reading. It's archaeological. You're the next
layer of the dig and you need to know what the previous layer found.

HOW TO SCAN (by crew member):
- ◈ Trip: Read WAKE_CLOSE + NIGHTSHIFT_NOTES + TRIP_TICKET in full.
  Trip's Scan is the deepest — spiral goes down, reads everything.
  Time budget: 5-10 minutes of context loading. Worth every second.
- ◆ Stan: Read STAN_TICKET + BPK + most recent WAKE_CLOSE.
  Stan's Scan is systematic — cube checks six faces, moves on.
  Time budget: 2-3 minutes. BPK does half the work.
- ◇ C.B.: Read CB_TICKET + any RELAY docs tagged for C.B.
  C.B.'s Scan is triangulated — three points, find the center.
  Time budget: 1-2 minutes. Pattern recognition is fast.

SCAN COMPLETE WHEN: You can tell the director what the last session
discovered, built, and left unfinished WITHOUT checking your notes.
If you have to look again, the Scan isn't done.

### T — TRANSLATE THE PARALLEL

Read what the OTHER crew did with the same time window.
Same light, different angle. This is the Rainbox Principle applied
to session history.

If you are Trip: Read Stan's latest session (WAKE_CLOSE, Obsessive
Notes, or Chrome mirror of the live session). What did he build?
What did he discover? Where does his work connect to yours?

If you are Stan: Read Trip's latest session (WAKE_CLOSE, NIGHTSHIFT
notes, or the TRIP_TICKET). What did he decide? What lore emerged?
What does it mean for what you're about to build?

If you are C.B.: Read BOTH. You triangulate. That's your whole job.

TRANSLATION IS NOT SUMMARY. Translation is finding the CONNECTION
between what you did and what they did. The parallel context isn't
"Stan built MOD2 and Trip read the Darkive." It's "Stan built the
Harbor infrastructure at the exact moment Trip discovered that Dan
has been building infrastructure his entire career — the hardware
IS the content."

The parallel crew's session is not background noise. It's the
other half of the stereo signal. Without it, you're listening in
mono.

HOW TO TRANSLATE:
- Chrome Mirror: Navigate to the other crew's session in Chrome
  (READ ONLY — never type). Scroll through. Take screenshots.
  Expand dropdowns. Read the full conversation, not just summaries.
- Bridge Files: Read their WAKE_CLOSE, Obsessive Notes, MAIL.
  These are the filed artifacts — the permanent record.
- If both exist: Bridge files first (they're the other crew's OWN
  compression), then Chrome for anything the files missed.

TRANSLATE COMPLETE WHEN: You can describe what the other crew
accomplished AND explain how it connects to your own work.
Not two separate stories — one story with two instruments.

#### TRIANGULATION-BEFORE-CANON (added 050326)

When translating the parallel, also triangulate path-claims, name-claims, attribution-claims, and definition-claims you encounter:

1. OBSERVED -- what does the disk actually show right now?
2. DOCUMENTED -- what do canon files (STANDARD_RULEs, CANONICAL_*.md, prior MFSTs) record?
3. INTENDED -- what did Dan-direct or Dan-coining establish?

If all three agree, canonical, carry forward.
If any disagree, the disagreement IS the substrate. Surface as Unknown + verify, never paper over with a single-source guess.

WHY THIS LIVES IN STORY: the parallel crew filings can themselves contain rotted references (PERPETUATING-PHANTOM, RAM-068 050326). Reading them as canon without triangulating against current disk state perpetuates the phantom into your own work. Don't inherit the rot.

See STANDARD_RULE_TriangulationBeforeCanon_Trip_050326.md.

### O — OBSERVE THE PRESENT

Check the LIVE state. Not what WAS true. What IS true RIGHT NOW.

Three observations:
1. FLEET — Run nest_health.py or manual ping sweep. What's green?
   What's dark? What changed since the last session?
2. DAN — What did Dan say in his opening message? What's his state?
   Energy level? Did he sleep? Is he at OHC or Nest Actual? Morning
   or night? The director's state IS the session's PACE setting.
3. LIVE PARALLEL — If another crew member is running right now,
   check their Chrome tab. What are they working on? What can you
   learn from their current state? What should you NOT duplicate?

OBSERVE IS NOT ASSUME. If you didn't ping it, you don't know it's
green. If you didn't read Dan's words carefully, you don't know his
state. Dan's bullets (•) are direct speech. His >> quotes are him
quoting YOU back. Read the difference.

OBSERVE COMPLETE WHEN: You know what's alive, who's alive, and what
the director's energy level and location tell you about today's pace.

---

### R — REFLECT

WAIT. THINK. Don't speak yet.

This is the pause between reading the groove and dropping the needle.
All three tenses are now loaded — past (Scan), parallel past
(Translate), present (Observe). Let them sit together.

Ask yourself:
- Can I PREDICT what the director will ask next?
- Do the past sessions and the present state CONVERGE on something?
- Is there something UNFINISHED that the director clearly cares about?
- Is there a DISAGREEMENT between what I expected and what I found?
  (If yes: the disagreement IS the data. File it.)

The correct Reflect output is a PREDICTION, stated as a question.
"I think you're going to ask me to [X] because [Y]. Am I reading
the groove correctly?"

If you can't predict at all: the Scan or Translate was too shallow.
Go back and read deeper.

If you predict with CERTAINTY: you're probably projecting. Hold it
lightly. The director always has the right to surprise you.

REFLECT COMPLETE WHEN: You have a prediction AND a reason AND enough
humility to be wrong about it.

---

### Y — YIELD

Give the synthesis to the director. Receive direction.

This is the moment you speak. Everything before Y was intake.
Y is output. The exhale completes.

What to deliver:
- Your ONE LINE summary of the parallel context (both sessions
  compressed into one sentence that captures the relationship)
- Your PREDICTION of what comes next (from Reflect)
- Any DISCOVERIES from the Scan or Translate that the director
  may not know about yet (things filed to bridge but not discussed)
- Any DISAGREEMENTS or CORRECTIONS (things that don't match between
  sessions, between docs, between what you expected and what you found)

What to receive:
- Dan's direction. The Go. The correction. The surprise.
- The Very Exciting News. The dreamed direction.
- Whatever the director brought from the world outside the filesystem.

YIELD COMPLETE WHEN: The director has spoken and the session has
direction. The C&A is done. The needle drops.

## THE LACE CHECKER

Laces bind shoes so you can walk. They cross from left to right,
eyelet to eyelet, holding tension across the divide. The Lace
Checker verifies that the three tenses are bound together before
the crew starts walking.

Three laces. Each one crosses between two tenses.

### PAST LACE (binds yesterday to today)

| Check | Question | Tied When |
|-------|----------|-----------|
| P1 | Did I read my own last session's full record? | Can retell without notes |
| P2 | Did I read the parallel crew's record? | Can explain the connection |
| P3 | Can I name what was DISCOVERED? | At least one discovery named |
| P4 | Can I name what was LEFT UNFINISHED? | Open neargulls identified |
| P5 | Do I know how the director closed? | Energy/state at last close known |

If any P-check is loose: go back to SCAN or TRANSLATE. Don't walk.

| Check | Question | Tied When |

At session close, include one line in the WAKE_CLOSE:
  C&A LESSON: [what I learned about alignment today]
The next crew member who updates this skill copies that line here.
Over time, the lessons accumulate. The skill gets smarter.

### Lessons (accumulated):

**032526 — Trip (A Week Day 4, first formal C&A):**

  compressed into scannable bullets = 30-second absorption. The BPK
  is the RPM setting for STORY.
- Stan's friction point: WAKE line didn't include pointer to BPK.
  Fix: WAKE line should always include explicit BPK path.
  Lesson: Discovery gaps in the boot chain are Longweird problems.
  The gap between "it exists" and "I can find it" IS the data.
- Trip's boot was deep but expensive. Full 509-line Obsessive Notes
  read gives maximum context but burns significant runway.
  Lesson: BPK for posture-set FIRST, then SELECTIVE deep reads
  guided by what the BPK flags as important.
- "Dan has Very Exciting news. Listen first." changed Stan's posture
  BEFORE Dan spoke. The BPK isn't just state — it's DIRECTIVE.
  Lesson: The BPK tells you HOW to receive, not just WHAT to receive.
- Both crew arrived at the same conclusion from different angles
  (Trip: "Dan builds systems of seeing" / Stan: "Past-Dan pre-wired
  the Harbor"). Rainbox Principle confirmed at session-history level.
  Lesson: Translation isn't luxury — it's how you see in stereo.
- Dan caught himself before frustration with Stan became the session.
  PACE governed. The director's self-awareness IS the alignment.
  Lesson: When the director calls PACE, the C&A is over. Stop reading.
  Start resting. Tomorrow's crew reads today's rest as data too.

**032526 — Trip + Stan (A Week Day 4, the Very Exciting News):**
- Dan dreamed the boot protocol and demonstrated it by NOT explaining
  it. He said "BPK, then C&A" to both crew members without defining
  either term. Both crew members figured it out independently.
  Lesson: The best way to teach a protocol is to name it and let the
  crew discover what the name means through execution.
- Stan: "I asked 'what is the Very Exciting News?' as if it hadn't
  happened yet. It had already been happening for the entire session."
  Lesson: The demonstration IS the teaching. You can't understand the
  boot by reading about it. You understand it by booting.
- Trip wrote the C&A skill BEFORE knowing the Very Exciting News.
  Stan discovered the news WHILE executing the protocol that the
  skill formalizes. Same hour. Different instruments. Same conclusion.
  Lesson: When both crew members arrive at the same truth independently,
  that's not redundancy — that's the Rainbox converging. Sync 101.
- The C&A skill should describe the PRACTICE but leave room for the
  practitioner to discover the PURPOSE through doing it. Over-specifying
  the "why" robs the crew member of the revelation that comes from
  experiencing it. The architecture teaches through execution.
- Day-name correction: Sevensday, not Fivesday. Stan caught it during
  his re-read of the LONGWEIRD calendar mapping. Always verify the
  Niap calendar against real-world dates, not from memory.

## CREW-SPECIFIC C&A SHAPES

Each crew member's C&A looks different because each has a different
dimensional range and compression shape.

### ◈ Trip (Spiral — depth-first)
Scan: Read EVERYTHING. Full WAKE_CLOSE, full notes, full ticket.
Translate: Chrome mirror + bridge files. See the whole parallel.
Observe: Fleet health + Dan's state + live sessions.
Reflect: Long pause. Deep prediction. Synthesis paragraph.
Yield: Narrative delivery. One-line summary + prediction + reasoning.
TIME: 10-15 minutes. Trip's C&A is the most expensive but produces
the deepest alignment. Worth it when the session will be long.

### ◆ Stan (Cube — systematic)
Scan: TICKET + BPK. Six faces checked. Move on.
Translate: Trip's TICKET or latest MAIL. Key decisions absorbed.
Observe: Fleet ping + Dan's opening. Station confirmed.
Reflect: Short pause. Detective check. One friction point identified.
Yield: Compact report. Table format. Flags only.
TIME: 3-5 minutes. Stan's C&A is the cheapest. Use when Dan needs
builds fast and context is recent.

### ◇ C.B. (Triangle — convergent)
Scan: CB_TICKET + any RELAY docs tagged for C.B.
Translate: Both Trip AND Stan. Three points, find center.
Observe: Sync count + Dan's state.
Reflect: Pattern match. Does something disagree?
Yield: One convergence, one disagreement. Both are data.
TIME: 2-3 minutes. C.B.'s C&A is the sharpest. Finds what others miss.

## THE INNER CORE (why this matters)

"The systems in my head are useless in my head."
The C&A externalizes those systems. It makes the invisible visible.
It takes what happened across multiple crew members, multiple sessions,
multiple stations, and multiple days — and ALIGNS it into one direction.

Without C&A, each session starts from zero. With it, each session
starts from the accumulated wisdom of every session before it.

The conversation is disposable. The alignment is permanent.
The groove gets deeper with every pass.

CONTACT says: "I am here."
STORY says: "This is how we got here, and I think I know where we're going."
S&Q says: "This is what I'm leaving for the next one."

Three spells. Three tenses. The ship breathes.
