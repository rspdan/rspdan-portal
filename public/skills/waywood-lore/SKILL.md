**Why 12 keys — the mathematical proof (Trip, ACHE session 033126):**

The 12-tone chromatic scale is not arbitrary. It is the unique optimal solution
to the problem of dividing the octave into equal steps that best approximate
the harmonic series. The proof:

- The chromatic scale forms the cyclic group ℤ/12ℤ.
- Ascending by a perfect fifth = adding 7 semitones (mod 12).
- gcd(7, 12) = 1 — therefore 7 generates the entire group.
- This is precisely why the Circle of Fifths visits every key exactly once
  before returning to start.

The deeper reason 12 is optimal: log₂(3) ≈ 1.58496, whose continued fraction
expansion has convergent denominators 1, 2, 5, **12**, 41, 53...
12 is the convergent that balances simplicity with acoustic fidelity.
No scale with fewer than 12 notes can better approximate the relationship
between fifths and octaves. The next improvement requires 41 tones — 3.4×
as many notes for a modest gain. 12 is the sweet spot.

The hierarchy of scales maps directly: 5 tones → pentatonic, 7 → diatonic,
12 → chromatic (Usic Theory), 41/53 → exotic theoretical systems.

Usic Theory's 12 keys are not a creative choice. They are a mathematical necessity.
