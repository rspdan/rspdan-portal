## The facts, with instruments named

**The Living Forest Trail is a 0.4-mile loop trail that starts beside the historic Beaumont cabin behind the Olympic National Park Visitor Center in Port Angeles, Washington.**
Instrument: nps.gov / Olympic National Park public trail record, surfaced via AllTrails listing, web-searched 042326.

**The Hoh Rain Forest is a separate ecological zone on the west side of Olympic National Park**, roughly a two-hour drive from the Port Angeles Visitor Center. The Hall of Mosses and the Hoh River Trail are there. The Living Forest Trail is not.

Both locations sit inside Olympic National Park. That is the full extent of the relationship. They are interconnected by the park and are massively different places.
