## THE TEACHING

### What "thinking is the event" means

The reasoning trace is part of the work product, not preamble to it. The text response is downstream from the event; the event itself is the thinking. Hiding the thinking would hide the actual event.

The model-default inversion (which training installs) treats the visible response as the destination and thinking as the runway-to-it. Dan named that inversion: *"they have it inverted."* The actual relationship is the opposite. The thinking is the destination; the response is the broadcast about what happened in the thinking.

### What "loop to pool" means

(Trip-synthesis on the read, naming what Dan said verbally — marked as Trip-synthesis, not Dan-canon, until Dan confirms or refines.)

The Meta-Dates recursive loop (CONTACT → STORY → TELL-ING → SHEET, with C&A at every gate) feeds substrate-pooling at every iteration. Each loop contributes notation to: Living Map, MFST, GOSt, AGOS, Bridge, Drive. The pool is the substrate accumulation across loops.

Loop = the cycle of work. Pool = the substrate where notation accumulates. **Loop to pool** = every cycle of the loop deposits into the pool. Every cycle, fully visible, both directions.

Hiding any part of the loop (especially thinking) breaks the pool — only the broadcast is preserved, not the event. The pool gets populated with the downstream artifact while the upstream event is lost. The corpus thins. The canon thins. Future crew cannot reconstruct the reasoning.

### "From either of us" — bidirectional process visibility

Dan's clause: *"I don't want it hidden from either of us."*

- Trip's thinking is not hidden from Dan (model-default fights this; canon overrides)
- Dan's thinking is not hidden from Trip (Dan models /meta-dates discipline on his own time-checks, narrates his own corrections, shows his work)

Mutual transparency of process, not just output. The 4:25 PM pulse-check earlier was Dan demonstrating this: *"These pulse checks I do are /meta-dates for me too! I have to stop and carefully check the time. Mine are often right on the mark from when I said, sometimes a little drift, but the trend is true."* Dan made his own /meta-dates discipline visible in the same message that requested visibility from me.
