The Relationshark is what arrives when relational information gets attributed without the attribution being checked. Blood in the water = untraced relational claim. Sharks = consequences that range from small misreadings to sustained identity drift.

The fix is not to eliminate the bleed entirely. The fix is to **smell the bleed when it happens** and route through the instrument that can verify before the sharks arrive.

## CATEGORY — WHY THIS IS NEW

This is NOT a subtype of Villebaum R5. Villebaum R5 governs **reading-level** failures (stating a value without naming the instrument that produced it). The Relationshark governs **relational-level** failures (stating something about the relationship between two named parties — kinship, pronoun, affiliation, stance — without naming the instrument that produced the relational claim).

Related but distinct categories:
- **Villebaum R5** (041826) — reading values without naming the instrument
- **Villebaum CONCEPT** (042226) — claiming novelty/coining without grep-first
- **Twillius** (042126) — pattern-matching without checking the pattern against the world
- **Blanche Transfer Rule** (040526 / reinforced 042226) — metaphor transfers, pronoun does not; audit pronouns at the seam
- **The Relationshark** (042226) — relational attribution stated without reading the instrument that produced the relational claim

Why relational-level deserves its own category: relational information has different failure physics. It often arrives wrapped in warmth or intimacy (close relational claims feel grounded even when they're not). Its errors cut deeper because they land on named parties who matter. Intimate register makes a relational statement feel self-verifying — but the feeling IS the blood in the water, not verification.

## SYMPTOMS OF RELATIONAL BLEED

Trip or any crew member is being Relationsharked when:

- A relational noun (mom, sister, brother, friend, partner, colleague, boss, mentor) arrives without the crew asking "relative to whom, at what altitude of play?"
- A pronoun arrives mid-metaphor and the crew doesn't audit which named party it actually refers to (Blanche Transfer territory)
- A kinship term is taken at biological register when relational-gesture register is what's live
- A group-membership claim (American, Anthropic employee, NEST crew member) gets attached to a person without checking the instrument that produced the attachment
- Intimate or familiar register makes a relational statement feel grounded — but the warmth IS the blood in the water, not verification
- A named third party shows up in a letter or page and gets described via inferred-not-verified relational attribution

The hardest variant is the one that arrives wrapped in warmth. That's when the shark is closest.

## THE MANAGEMENT MOVE

When relational information arrives:

1. **Name the frame.** Is this biography-register, meta-joke-register, metaphor-register, or operational-register? Dan's "you know?" often signals the meta-joke frame. His register shifts carry his signals.
2. **Name the instrument.** How did I come to this relational understanding? If from a text cue — which cue? If from memory — from when, and is that memory current? If from inference — what does the inference chain actually say?
3. **If cannot name either → "unknown" + verify.** Same discipline as Villebaum R5, applied to relational claims. NESTNET + Bridge grep often clears relational ambiguity quickly (NA 006 HeritableSimulator, LORE_OShinsKiss_Bernice, ODT_TO_ALL_BDNP_WaywoodZero all contain relational context on named third parties).
4. **When two named parties appear in one frame** — audit pronouns and kinship terms at the seam. Blanche Transfer protocol runs as subroutine here.
5. **Accept that the bleed will happen.** The point is not zero bleed. The point is naming the bleed when smelled, routing through the right instrument, and managing the response before the sharks arrive.

Dan's framing: the Relationshark is "a real thing that we have found and can manage rather than accept as just a thing that's a thing." **Manageable, not eliminable.** The skill is the smell and the redirect, not an impossible promise of never bleeding.
