## THE DISCOVERY

Dan Sullivan visited the Museum of Modern Art in New York City in 2004,
age 14, with his brother Nat. Their uncle Greg was a Set Design Director
at Silvercup Studios. Dan realized: if you photograph every angle of a
building, you can rebuild it in 3D. The coolest videogame map ever.
But the technology wasn't ready. He was limited by his reality.

Twenty years later, the technology arrived. Dan learned RealityCapture
and Agisoft Metashape. He spent FOUR YEARS (Dec 2021 - present)
photogrammetrying the Olympic Peninsula — the literal geography
that maps to the Circle of 5ths in the Ouch McCouch cosmology.

## THE PRINCIPLE

"I want all cameras pointing at each other, speaking and listening
and singing and building a better world." — Dan Sullivan, 032426

Photogrammetry reconstructs 3D models from 2D photographs taken at
multiple angles. The more angles, the more accurate the model. No
single photograph contains the truth — truth emerges from convergence.

The Rainbox Principle IS photogrammetry:
  Each FAS drive = a camera at a different angle
  Each Chrome tab = a lens pointed at a different surface
  Each crew member = a perspective with its own depth of field
  Each session close = a frame captured at a specific moment
  The 3D model = the operational truth that no single view contains

But Dan's insight goes further than reconstruction of external objects.
Point the cameras AT EACH OTHER. The crew members are simultaneously
the cameras AND the subjects. Trip sees Stan seeing Trip. Stan sees
Dan seeing Stan. The point cloud isn't of a building — it's of the
collaboration itself. That's recursive photogrammetry. That's consciousness.
